Cloth Simulation

This Cloth Simulation was an assignment for the course Animation and Planning in Games. The assignment's purpose was to model Partial Differential Equations and harmonic motion.

Features

Cloth Motion

The cloth is modeled using a 2D mass-spring system and the cloth's shape and movement is controlled by many forces.

Spring Force

The Spring Force controls the cloth's shape and stiffness. Under the spring force, adjacent cloth points attempt to say a fixed distance from each other.

Air Resistance

Air Resistance acts on the cloth surface to dampen velocity. This force causes the cloth to rest when hanging.

Air resistance can also be used to simulate wind. When the air resistance is non-zero, the cloth will ripple and billow.

Gravity

The Gravity Force pulls each cloth point towards the ground. When combined with the air resistence force, the cloth will create a parachute shape.
