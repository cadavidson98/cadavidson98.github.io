Pathtacer

This pathtracer was a final project for the course Fundamentals of Computer Graphics. I partnered with another student, Hailin Archer, to upgrade our raytracer from a previous assignment into a pathtracer for more photo-realistic rendering.

Features

Compared to our initial raytracers, this pathtracer added several enhanced rendering features. These features all stemmed from the integrating the light-transport equation in favor of using traditional local illumination models, such as Phong-Blinn.

Soft Shadows & Ambient Occulusion

Instead of a harsh difference between light and shadow, shadows now have blurrier, softer edges. This is because shadowed areas now receive indirect lighting.

Raytracing the Stanford bunny. Shadows are very sharp.

Pathtracing the Stanford bunny. Shadows are much softer.

On a global scale, this behavior also simulates Ambient Occulusion.

Color Bleed

When light rays travel through the scene, they tend to accumulate the color of any surfaces they touch. This effect is called color bleeding and is noticeable when 2 objects are nearby.

Caustics

When light passes through refractive materials, such as glass or water, the light bends and focuses onto a particular point.
